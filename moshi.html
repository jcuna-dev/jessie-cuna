My dearest Earleen Rose,

There is a kind of love that doesn't announce itself — it arrives quietly, like the first light of morning that you almost miss because you're still half asleep. That is how you came into my life. And now, one year and five months later, I find myself waking every day in disbelief that someone as radiant as you chose to love someone like me.

You carry your name beautifully — Earleen Rose — and I have come to believe it was no accident. You are soft and lovely like a rose in full bloom, but you have thorns too: a quiet strength, a fierce heart, a depth that the casual eye might miss entirely. I have never wanted to miss a single thing about you.

· · · ♥ · · ·

The way your presence fills a room before you even speak. The warmth you carry so effortlessly — not just for me, but for everyone lucky enough to be near you. I notice how genuinely you feel things, how you love with intention, how you make ordinary moments feel like they deserve to be remembered. And I have been trying to remember all of them.

On days when life felt heavy, you were the reason I kept going. Not because you fixed anything — but because being near you reminded me that beauty still exists, that gentleness is real, and that I am seen by someone whose seeing matters most to me.

· · · ♥ · · ·

So on this monthsary — our — I do not simply want to celebrate how far we've come. I want to celebrate you. The way you love, the way you laugh, the way your name sounds when I say it and it still feels like poetry. You are my favorite poem, Earleen Rose. One I will never stop reading.

I choose you again today. I will choose you in every quiet Tuesday and every ordinary evening. You are not just my love — you are my home.

Yours completely and always, JC
P.S. — Your name has two beautiful words in it and somehow they still aren't enough to describe you. I love you. ♥
